In Japanese kissatens (traditional coffeehouses), dark roast coffee is often brewed using the “nel drip” method, a slow and meticulous process that brings out deep, rich flavors. The name comes from the flannel filter used, which allows the coffee grounds to steep longer, creating a smoother, fuller-bodied cup. This method, cherished for decades, is considered an art form, requiring precision and patience. Try this iconic method at home with our darkest roast yet, Tokyo Kissa. You can also experience it at one of 10 cafes in Southern California that offer the nel drip technique.
